DKDS: A Benchmark Dataset of Degraded Kuzushiji Documents with Seals for Detection and Binarization

Rui-Yang Ju, Hirotaka Kameko, Kohei Yamashita, Shinsuke Mori
Kyoto University
Paper arXiv Code Dataset
DKDS dataset is the first collection of degraded ancient Japanese document images specifically designed to address the challenge of Kuzushiji characters overlapping with seals. Based on the dataset, we define two benchmark tracks: (1) Text and Seal Detection, and (2) Document Binarization.

Motivation

Within ancient Japanese documents, seals appear as red marks that frequently overlap with Kuzushiji characters. This overlap can blur character boundaries and significantly reduce OCR accuracy. From left to right, the observed OCR errors include: recognition of extra characters, incorrect character recognition, and misidentification of seal carvings as text. OCR was conducted using the “miwo” application [Clanuwat et al. 2021], and binarization was performed according to the method proposed by [Ju et al. 2024].

Workflow

The overall workflow of the proposed DKDS dataset construction, including detection annotations, initial binarization ground-truth generation, verification, and manual correction. The initial binarization ground-truth was generated using the method of [Ju et al. 2024], while the verification was conducted by the trained Kuzushiji expert.
